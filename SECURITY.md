# Security Policy

## Our Commitment
Security and anonymity are the core pillars of GHOST-PARTISAN. We take all security reports seriously and aim to address confirmed vulnerabilities as quickly as possible to protect our users.

## Reporting a Vulnerability
**Please do not report security vulnerabilities via public GitHub issues.** If you believe you have found a security vulnerability, please report it to us privately via email at:
**partisan.dev@proton.me**

### What to include in your report:
- A brief summary of the potential impact.
- Step-by-step instructions (or a script/PoC) to reproduce the issue.
- The version of GHOST you are using.

## Responsible Disclosure Guidelines
To protect the community, we ask that you follow these guidelines:
1. **Confidentiality:** Give us a reasonable amount of time (typically 90 days) to fix the issue before sharing any information publicly.
2. **No Exploitation:** Do not attempt to access user data or disrupt the service beyond what is necessary to prove the vulnerability.
3. **Safe Harbor:** If you act in good faith and follow this policy, we pledge not to pursue any legal action against you.

## Recognition
While we do not currently have a formal bug bounty program, we are happy to credit researchers in our release notes (with your permission) for significant findings that help improve the safety of GHOST-PARTISAN.

*Note: For general bugs that do not pose a security risk, please use the standard Issue Tracker.*
